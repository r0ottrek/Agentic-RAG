# Starting My Cybersecurity Journey

If you're reading this, you're probably either curious about my journey or thinking about starting your own path into cybersecurity. Either way, welcome. Let me tell you why I'm doing this and how I'm planning to get there. 
 Why Cybersecurity? 
 I've always been the person who wanted to know how things work. Not just use them—actually understand them. When I was younger, that meant taking apart electronics to see what was inside. Now it means understanding how systems communicate, where the vulnerabilities are, and how to protect (or exploit) them. 
 Cybersecurity felt like the natural evolution of that curiosity. It's a field where you never stop learning because the landscape is always changing. New vulnerabilities, new attack vectors, new defenses. It's a puzzle that never gets solved. 
 The Plan 
 I'm not going the traditional college route. Instead, I'm building my skills through: 
 Certifications  - Starting with A+, then Network+, Security+, and beyond 
 Hands-on practice  - Home lab, CTFs, vulnerable VMs 
 Real experience  - Running my own IT services business 
 Documentation  - This blog and my YouTube channel 
 The goal is to build a portfolio that speaks louder than a degree. Show what I can do, not just what I've been taught. 
 Where I Want to End Up 
 Long term? I want to be doing penetration testing or security research. Finding vulnerabilities before the bad guys do. But I know that takes time and experience, so I'm starting with the fundamentals and working my way up. 
 For now, I'm focused on learning everything I can, helping people with their tech problems, and documenting the journey along the way. 
 If you're on a similar path, I'd love to connect. We're all figuring this out together.
